Name: Goat
Type: Neutral (rams occasionally)
Category: Animal

Health: 10 HP (5 hearts)
Attack Damage: 3 (normal ram) / 5 (screaming goat ram)
Armor: 0
Movement Speed: 0.40
XP Dropped: 1-3

Spawn Locations:
- Frozen Peaks
- Jagged Peaks
- Stony Peaks
- Meadow (rarely)
- Grove (rarely)

Drops:
- Nothing (milk with Bucket — Goat Milk identical to Cow Milk)

Breeding:
- Feed 2 Goats Wheat

Variants:
- Normal Goat (98%)
- Screaming Goat (2% — makes exaggerated screaming sounds, rams more frequently)

Special Abilities:
- Rams players and mobs randomly (long charging animation before impact)
- Rams hard blocks or entities when charging — if hits a hard block, drops Goat Horn
- Goat Horn: When blown, makes a raid horn sound — various types based on which block it hits
  (can be found in various types: Ponder, Sing, Seek, Feel, Admire, Call, Yearn, Dream)
- Extra long jump height (10 blocks!) — traverses mountain terrain easily
- Screaming Goats ram far more often than normal goats
- Does not take fall damage when landing on blocks designated safe
- Can be milked with Bucket

Goat Horn Types (dropped when ramming different hardness blocks):
- Different sounds based on block type rammed (wood vs stone etc.)

Behavior:
- Wanders mountain terrain
- Occasionally charges at player or mobs
- Jumps huge distances to cross terrain
- Screaming goat does all normal sounds but louder/distorted

Sounds: Bleating normally / Screaming Goat: extremely loud, comical scream

Notes:
- Screaming Goat sounds became a major internet meme before/after Minecraft 1.17
- Goat Horns are used in Raids sound signaling (cosmetic use)
- The extremely long jump makes them impressive to watch on cliff edges
- Added in 1.17 as part of the mountain biome overhaul
- Goats falling off cliffs is a common and comedic occurrence
